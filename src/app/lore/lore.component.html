History

The Creation

First came The Fathers, Marr and Innoruk. These great spirits, born from the stars, arrived on Solusek and began their dominion.

Marr created the races of Human, Elf, Halfling, Dwarf, Giant and Fey, as well as the spirits Tir, Sen and Hep to watch over them.

Innoruk created the races of Iksar, Troll, Ogre, Goblin and Fel, as well as the spirits Vis, Gek and Dun to guide them.

For centuries the races developed in isolation, the Fey holding dominion over the east and the Fel over the west. However as time went on and their reach spread, the races collided and due to their differences small battles over territory naturally erupted.

The Great Separation

In the Elf city of Lunathil, a young Prince named Elias Erudar, destined to never become king (due to his 3 older brothers), grew ambitious. A Fel Lord by the name of Azureal took advantage of Elias' growing disobedience and offered him power in return for the betrayal of his kind.

Azureal, along with Elias and the prince's loyal followers, struck at the heart of the elves, slaying Elias' mother Queen Illiana. This drove the elves into civil war and eventually separation.

Elias and his followers were marked by the Fel Lord Azureal and along with granting them extra physical abilities, this gradually altered their appearance and gave them a distaste for sunlight. They became the first Dark Elves and built a new underground home, known as the Underdark.

While the Elven shamans and rangers detested the actions of Elias and his followers, they wanted neither vengeance or war, and left Lunathil, retreating into The Endless Forest and creating the tree-city Erethil as the first Wood Elves.

Elias' brothers and the remaining elves in Lunathil tried to rebuild order, vowing to take vengeance on Elias. The ensuing battles between what were now known as the High Elves and the Dark Elves resulted in the deaths of many and eventually Elias was slain by his older brother Kael, who became King of the High Elves. The hatred between the High Elves and Dark Elves remained however, and the battles continued.

The Great War

The Fey, a race that valued life above all else, finally grew sick of the squabbles of what they considered the "lesser races", and constructed the first rift to another, unpopulated world, and left Solusek not to be seen or heard from since.

The Fel, always looking for their chance to seize power, took this opportunity to strike. They rallied the western armies under the banner of Innoruk to strike at the east.

The Iksar initially resisted, not liking the idea of bowing to the Fel, however to prove a point the Fel laid waste to their home (now known as 'Old Cabilis'), and after being forced to a new home, the Iksar finally had to succumb to their Fel lords.

The armies of Marr knew that they must fight back together or be overwhelmed, and so The Great War began. Many blamed the war on the Fey for abandoning the ideals of Marr; others simply blamed the Fel for their insatiable hunger for power at all costs.

The war raged for 1000 years; death and destruction unmeasurable across generations and generations.

Peace at last

60 years ago, champions from each side - the Human Archmage Belgarath and the Dark Elf Warlock Anathus - began peace talks.

Both powerful and wise spellcasters, the champions saw the Fel as the plague that caused the war and decided to combine their power to create a giant rift. The rift was a link to a faraway world, which they forced the Fel through and closed it for good.

Alas, peace takes time to proliferate, and the battles between the Dark Elves and the Stone Guard of Sherridon continued for years after, before finally ending in betrayal. A member of the Stone Guard, mistakenly betrayed his own, leading to a slaughter that is now known as The Night of the Long Knives.

Since that night peace between the races has held, though recovering from such a war is not easy and bad blood between the races still runs deep. A generation left in the shadow of the war, trying to rebuild in a world full of poverty, crime, and distrust.

The Pantheon

The gods are split into 2 main sects, knows as the Fathers. Beneath each of these are the Lesser Spirits. Since the end of the war, diversity of beliefs has somewhat increased, however historically your race has dictated which Father you worship (if any).

Innoruk - Father of Chaos

Innoruk represents chaos, his followers believing that the greatest civilizations are developed through freedom and natural selection, rather than law and order.

Vis - Spirit of Power

Vis represents the hunt for power at all costs. Followers see the furthering of ones own power as their primary goal in life.

Gek - Spirit of Growth

Gek represents the idea of growth in all things, respecting the concepts of improving oneself and the society around them.

Dun - Spirit of Death

Dun represents the inevitability of death, and followers have great reverence for the passing between the worlds of living and the dead.

Marr - Father of Order

Marr represents order, his followers believing that firm law and order is required to ensure that all citizens are treated equally and fairly.

Tir - Spirit of Pride

Tir represents pride for oneself and others, and the idea of nurturing family.

Sen - Spirit of Honour

Sen represents honour in all things, followers strongly believe in their personal code of honour and rarely stray from it.

Hep - Spirit of Wisdom

Hep represents wisdom and the thirst for knowledge. Followers are usually found in libraries, improving themselves through learning.

Non Playable Races

These are the races mentioned in lore that are not playable.

Fey

The Fey are the most powerful and loved creation of Marr. They respect life above all else due to the fact they live many years, and therefor each death is seen as a great loss. This is why they could not bear to coexist with the other races, who did not respect life in the same way, and decided to leave Solusek.

Ironically, the Fey leaving caused more death and destruction in the form of The Great War, and they have not been seen or heard from since. Previously they lived in the now High Elf city of Lunathil.

The Fey are similar in appearance to High Elves until maturity when they develop angelic wings. Ancient Fey are also said to have a halo-like glow around their brow.

Fel

The Fel, also known as demons, are seen as the most evil of all the races due to their need to feed on the spirit of other creatures (including other Fel). All Fel grow up in a very survive or die mentally due to the threat of being consumed by others of their kind. They have no notion of family or loyalty but to follow a hierarchy governed by power and fear.

The Fel originally lived near the location of the now Underdark, they have not been seen since they were banished at the end of the Great War.

Fel take on the physical characteristics of creatures they consume, which usually results in odd looking combinations, however the one common trait is their sharp yellow eyes.

Goblins

Simple in desire but cunning in execution, goblins were affected greatly by the Great War. Seen by the armies of Innoruk as fodder, Goblins now have a lingering distrust for all other races. They tend to keep to themselves, living in small tribes scattered across the land.

Goblins are similar in size to Halflings, however are slightly more slender and have the green skin of Trolls.

Giants

A result of living a long, slow life, Giants rarely procreate and as a result are quite rare. They do not tend to stay together in large groups and usually roam individually or as a single family unit. Similar to Goblins, Giants tend to avoid the other races after being hunted for their incredibly strong bones.

Most giants reside in the deserts southeast of Qur'Atil, though they can be found wandering across the land.

Giants vary largely in size and usually stand anywhere between 20' and 50' tall. The largest on record is Briggs 'The Collosus' who stood at 58'.

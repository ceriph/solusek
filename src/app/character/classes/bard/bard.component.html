Bard

It might not seem conventional to bring a guitar to a sword fight, but once you see a bard at work you will understand. They use their gifts of music to weave magical sonnets, bolstering their allies and weakening their foes. Rock on.

Hit Die

d8

Important stats

Charisma, Agility, Strength

Starting equipment

Damaged Leather Armour, Damaged Short Sword, choose either a hand drum, a flute, or a lute

Proficiencies

Light Armour, Simple Weapons, Small Weapons, Medium Weapons, Ranged Weapons, Small Shields

Skills

A lot of bard skills have the type song, these remain active until the bard switches to another song or becomes incapacitated. The bard should keep track of which song is active, and check for any effects that trigger at the start of their turn.

Fast Hands

Passive - Bards can inexplicably play a musical instrument in their off hand while wielding a weapon in their main hand. Even if the instrument requires two hands. Don't question it.

Invigorating Warsong

Action - song - while active, all allies within earshot gain +1 to all attack rolls. Requires an instrument.

Deafening Shout

Action - once per encounter - attack vs CHA on enemy within earshot, if it hits the enemy takes 1d6+CHA damage and is knocked down

Chords of Agony

Action - song - attack vs CHA on all enemies within earshot, if it hits each enemy takes 1d4+CHA now and must defend the attack at the start of the bards turn while the song is active. Requires an instrument.

Extra Attack

Passive - once per round - the character can perform an additional basic attack with their main weapon

Hymn of Restoration

Action - song - all allies within earshot gain 1d4+CHA health now at the start of the bards turn while the song is active

Charm

Action - once per day - attack vs CHA, if it hits the enemy is charmed until the end of their next turn.

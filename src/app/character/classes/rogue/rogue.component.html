Rogue

Rogues like to stay in the shadows, remaining hidden and striking enemies from behind. Thought rogues like to be up close with enemies to do large amounts of damage, they can't afford to take many hits.

Hit Die

d6

Important stats

Agility, Intelligence, Strength

Starting equipment

Damaged Leather Armour, Damaged Dagger, Lockpick

Proficiencies

Light Armour, Simple Weapons, Small Weapons

Skills

Backstab

Passive - The rogue deals an additional 1d4 damage when attacking a target from behind with their main weapon (increases to 2d4 at level 5, 3d4 at level 10, 4d4 at level 15, and 5d4 at level 20). Piercing weapon required.

Thievery

Passive - the rogue receives +1 to any checks relating to lockpicking or pickpocketing (increases to +2 at level 5, +3 at level 10, +4 at level 15 and +5 at level 20).

Apply Weak Poison

Action - once per encounter - infuse your weapon with poison, any enemies that are hit must make a CON saving throw vs 15 or they will be poisoned, making a saving throw each round or suffering 1d6 damage.

Shadow Step

Action - once per encounter - attack INT vs INT on an enemy target within 10m, if successful, the rogue moves immediately behind the target and can perform a single attack with their main weapon. If it fails, the rogue moves behind the target but cannot perform an attack.

Dual Wield

Passive - The character may now equip a small weapon in their off-hand, and can make an additional basic attack with that weapon once per round.

Smoke Bomb

Action - once per encounter - creates a field of smoke on a target 3m wide (9x9 grid), all characters within that area other than the rogue suffer -3 to all attack rolls.

Vanish

Action - once per encounter - the rogue becomes invisible until the end of their next turn. They may still move and attack while this is active.

Extra Attack

Passive - once per round - the character can perform an additional basic attack with their main weapon
